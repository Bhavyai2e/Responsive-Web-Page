What to do if...

Select the topic below to see what you should do in each scenario.

If you are in a meeting and encounter a potential WRD external opportunity of interest, make sure to note down all key details. You should assess the opportunity based on alignment with WRD's current goals and values. After the meeting, reach out to the WRD business development team to discuss the opportunity and see if it warrants further investigation or collaboration.

To understand WRD's partnering areas of interest, consult the WRD strategic documents or reach out to the partnerships team. WRD typically focuses on areas that align with its mission of advancing innovative research and technologies. Common areas of interest may include but are not limited to biomedical research, sustainable solutions, and emerging digital technologies. Contact the partnerships team for the most updated list of focus areas.

If a non-employee approaches you with a potential WRD external opportunity, handle the conversation professionally and avoid making any commitments. Gather as much information as possible about the opportunity and express your interest. Then, forward the details to the WRD business development or partnerships team for a formal review. Ensure that confidentiality and internal protocols are followed when sharing sensitive information.
